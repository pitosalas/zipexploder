General thought: I think the value proposition canvas is a great idea to sketch startups' initial thoughts and inspirations. Having the canvas can further benefit later processes including making more complicated tables, databases, prototyping, etc. After all, it is crucial to identify the most accurate market fit -- so your needs and customers match!

I think the gain and pain sections (in both the value proposition section and customer section) are pretty much overlapping. They all address how goods & services can make people's lives easier by solving some problems. By explaining how to solve a problem one basically covers what benefits the goods & services are introducing. Thus. the two sections might be overlapping.

From Keurig, I learned that there is one way to keep customers paying -- set up an initial amount, and then the smaller yet necessary "subscriptions" to keep the service going in a customer's LTV.
