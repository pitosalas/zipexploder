When using a business model that revolves around forcing customers to buy another product to make your product useful like razors and blades, it is better to have the "blade" product be something that the user wants to experience via your product rather than needs to make your product work. On paper, these are the same thing, as the product hinges on the purchase of another to be useful. In practice, however, the user associates positive feelings with wanting to get something rather than needing to.
